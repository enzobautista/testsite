An Analysis of the Efficient Market Hypothesis
What is the Efficient
Market Hypothesis?
The Efficient Market Hypothesis, commonly known as the random walk theory, suggests that the current stock market prices, reflected through the stock market indices, are an accurate reflection of information about the market. The theory suggests that fluctuations in the stock market are caused by market stakeholders who base their decisions from the available information.
The project will analyze world news using sentiment analysis and see if the sign of news, like signs in math, denote positivity or negativity. The project will juxtapose major world news events and their respective sign rank with major movements in the Dow Jones Industrial Average, a stock market index created by Charles Dow.
Methodology
In order to determine whether the news depicted as positive or negative, the project will use word-sentiment dictionaries.
This will be done by counting the number of positive words found inside the news articles of each day.
The result (score) will then be compared / juxtaposed to the values of the Dow Jones Index
Correlation between news reports (both positive and negative) and dips in the stock market will be determined using regression.
